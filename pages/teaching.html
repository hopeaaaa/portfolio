Science World

Tech-Up is Science World’s province-wide initiative that empowers educators to bring coding, computational thinking, and digital skills into the classroom. As part of this program, I developed and delivered technical curriculum and digital learning resources for K–12 educators across the Lower Mainland. My work focused on translating computational concepts into hands-on, creative coding experiences using tools such as Micro:bit, Scratch, and HTML/CSS, while supporting teachers in confidently integrating technology into diverse learning environments.

Crofton House School

At Crofton House School, a private K–12 school for girls, I worked as the Makerspace Technician and Design-Thinking Facilitator, where I planned, equipped, and launched the school’s first makerspace. I established workflows for fabrication and prototyping using 3D printing, laser cutting, Arduino, and digital modeling tools, and provided technical support and training for educators implementing design-thinking and STEAM-based learning initiatives.
